Marketing Tactics

The Seven P's of Services Marketing

Strategic marketing actions to promote your service!

Product

In services the product is intangible and refers to the service itself.

Decisions to consider:

  • Supporting facilities needed to create a service
  • Directly observable benefits the customers need
  • Psycological benefits the customers need

A hotels product would be the guests hotel room as well as the hotels service processes.

Place

Where a service is consumed which is often the location of the service.

Decisions to consider:

  • The area surrounding the physcial location
  • Customers ability to easily access the service both physically and digitally

For a hotel this could be either the physical location of the hotel or the webiste the custome visited to book their stay.

Price

Seen as an indicator of quality for services. Consumers compare the price of the service to the value they receive.

Decisions to consider:

  • Customers arrive at quality perceptions on the basis of price.
  • Different customers pay different prices for service depending on experience.
  • Non-monetary aspects to consider when determining price such as the cost of time.

The price of a hotel room will vary according to specific features of the room such as the size, number of beds and the view.

Promotion

Refers to the entire set of activities which communicate, infrom and advertise the service to customers.

Decisions to consider:

  • Distribute content to make services tangible
  • Correct strategies to target your customers

A hotels promotion includes the digial and phsycial advertising of their hotel with content that makes ther service tagible in the eyes of the customer such as a picture of their room or the pool.

People

All human actors who play a part in service delivery.

Decisions to consider:

  • Services require employee contact
  • Employee recruitment and motivations
  • Customer education and training

The people in a hotel that interact with guests include managers, receptionists, cleaners and more.

Process

The actual procedures, mechanisms, and flow of activities by which the service is delivered - the service delivery and operating systems.

Decisions to consider:

  • Standardized or customized flow of activities
  • Number of steps (Simple vs. complex)
  • Customer Involvement

The key process for a hotel includes when a guest books the room, checks in to their room, stays in the hotel and when they check out from the hotel.

Physical Evidence

The environment in which the service is delivered and where the employees and customer interact and any tangible components that facilitate performance or communication of the services.

Decisions to consider:

  • Facility design, equipment, and signage
  • Web pages, business cards, invoices, etc.

A hotels physical evidence includes the hotels website as well as the lobby and the music playing.
