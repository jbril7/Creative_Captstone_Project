What is Market Segmentation?

Market segmentation is the process of dividing a target market into smaller, more defined categories. It segments customers into groups that share similar characteristics such as demographics, interests, needs, or location.

When should you use market segmentation?

  • Customers cannot be reached effectively with the same marketing strategy
  • There are identifiable and divisible segments
  • You must be able to compare the market segments in terms of sales potential, costs and profits
  • One segment must have enough profit potential to justify developing a special marketing mix
  • Differentiate you service from competitors
  • Identify the most effective marketing tactics

How do you determine which segmentation variable to use for your service?

  • Brainstorm and identify marketing objectives
  • Identify factors which lead to customers use of the service
  • Observe how the service fills customers needs

What is Market Targeting?

Market Targeting is the process of determining which segment to focus your marketing efforts on.

Targeting Strategies


Targeting Strategy Description Example
Mass Marketing
(undifferentiated marketing). 		designs a single marketing mix and directs it at the entire market for a particular product; effective for homogenous markets Television Ad
Concentrated Marketing
(Niche marketing)		 Targets single market segment using one marketing mix Honey Moon Destination
Segmented Marketing
(Differentiated Marketing) 		Targets two or more segments by developing a marketing mix for each segments with separate offers for each Family Hotel

How should you choose your target market and strategies?

  • Target segmented audiences you can speak to directly with a focused marketing message
  • Personalized marketing that creates a personal connection with the customer and your service to increase customer loyalty

What is Market Positioning?

Market Positioning is the process of establishing and maintaining a distinctive place in the market that is most likely to appeal to the selected audience and set your service apart from the competition.

Positioning Strategies


Positioning Strategy Description Example
Competitor-Based Positioning Show your customers how you are better than your competitors Better views from your hotel room or the unique attributes of the pool area
Benefit-based positioning Show your customers the benefits that they will recieve from your service Family relaxing by the pool

Positioning Strategy = Market Segmentation + Target Market

  • Determine most effective strategy to differentiate your service from other competitors in the eyes of the customer
  • Consistent brand message
  • Creates foundation for how you will market your service
